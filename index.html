Who I Am

Security isn't optional

Photo
coming soon

Security is one of those things that tends to feel abstract until it's knocking on your front door. I've spent the better part of a decade trying to decode incidents like those before they ever arrive. With degrees in Information Science, a cybersecurity certificate, a Certified AI Consultant credential, and five years of hands-on industry work, I've sharpened the core skill of knowing exactly how, when, and where things break. I wanted to make sure that security and protection didn't stop where the corporation walls began.

So, when I left, I started to choose who I was protecting.

I now sit in the grey area between AI and cybersecurity, and a large part of what I do now is talk to people about the reality of both: what AI is, what it can actually do, where it falls short, and what you might be accidentally handing over when you use it.

Cybersecurity is a similar conversation: what is security hygiene, data privacy, understanding fundamentals, and everything in between. We go through it together at your pace, writing down the processes that live in the walls of your mind, building a baseline of understanding, and figuring out what security looks like for you specifically. There's a lot of noise out there and not enough honesty, so I'd rather give you the version that's actually useful, even if it's less of the glitz and glamour.

Security only works best when you understand it yourself. It's up to you to make security a guarantee.

Let's make your security a priority.
